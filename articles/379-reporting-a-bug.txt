Please [contact us through the Pokémon GO app](https://niantic.helpshift.com/hc/en/6-pokemon-go/faq/2030-how-to-contact-support/?p=web&s=get-in-touch&f=how-to-contact-support) to report a potential bug that is not already listed on [the Known Issues page](https://niantic.helpshift.com/hc/en/6-pokemon-go/section/180-release-notes-known-issues/).  **You won't get an individual response to your report** , but we’ll use this information to help us reproduce the issue and identify trends.

## What happens after I file a report?

* We review bug reports to find trends and uncover recurring issues within Pokémon GO. Our Quality Assurance (QA) team compiles similar reports to gather common factors and scenarios
* Once we define the scope of the issue, the team designs tests to reproduce the issue.
* After QA determines the conditions to reproduce the issue, engineers work on resolving the issue.
* Before the resolution is implemented into the app, QA does a final review to ensure that the resolution fully resolves the issue and does not introduce additional issues.
* If the resolution passes review, the change is added to an upcoming release of the app.

## Should I file a bug report?

Before submitting a technical problem, please ensure you are running the latest version of Pokémon GO on a supported device. If you’re using an out-of-date version, please upgrade the app and see if the issue persists.

## What should I include in my bug report?

* A clear and concise description of the issue.
* Describe what happened and what you expected to happen.
* Steps to reproduce the issue, if available.
* Screenshots of the issue, if relevant.
* Any error messages you received.
